With $ you get formulas like \(1\;\textrm {Hz}=\frac {1}{\textrm {s}}\) and \( x =\frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}}\) just within the text,

and with $$ ... $$ you get formulas in so-called "display mode", centered on a line, like

\[ 1\;\textrm {Hz}=\frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \text { and } x =\frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \]

or better with \[ ..\] like

\[ 1\;\textrm {Hz}=\frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \text { and } x =\frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \]

With aligned you can align (here on ’=’): \( \begin {aligned} 1\; \textrm {Hz} & = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x & = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \\ \end {aligned} \),

and with align you can also do that (in display mode)

\begin{align} 1\; \textrm {Hz} & = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x & = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \end{align}

and with a final \\ this becomes

\begin{align} 1\; \textrm {Hz} & = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x & = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \\ \end{align}

or with align* (no tags)

\begin{align*} 1\; \textrm {Hz} & = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x & = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \end{align*}

You could also use equation (MIND THE HTML-PDF difference ?)

\begin{equation} 1\; \textrm {Hz} = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \\ \int _0^\infty \frac {1}{x} = \ldots \\ \int _0^\infty \frac {1}{x^2} = \ldots \end{equation}

or equation*

\begin{equation*} 1\; \textrm {Hz} = \frac {1}{\textrm {s}} \\ 1 + x = \frac {\frac 1x}{1+\frac {1}{x}} \\ \int _0^\infty \frac {1}{x} = \ldots \\ \int _0^\infty \frac {1}{x^2} = \ldots \end{equation*}
